A photo essay featuring four families who we have worked with. Photographs and essay by Anna Low-Beer.

Being homeless didn't stop this middle school student from making honor roll and becoming debate chamption. The City Bar Justice Center helped get her essentials such as the laptop needed for her advanced classes and warm winter clothing. After two years in a shelter, she and her mother recently moved into a private dwelling.

As profiled in The New York Times

The New York Times has featured a few of the families featured in the photo story.

After fleeing domestic violence, our client ended up in a homeless shelter with her autistic son who was allergic to the infested rooms at the shelter. Since this article was published, she has moved into a two-bedroom apartment and her child has educational toys and piano keyboard to help him thrive. She continues to host a public-access TV show.

After doctors in her home country were unable to care for their granddaughter's special needs, the grandparents took her to the U.S. but they ended up. At one point they were living in a shelter two hours away from her school, but advocacy by pro bono lawyers and the City Bar Justice Center got them transferred her to a much closer shelter. The grandmother has received training and found work as a home health aide.
