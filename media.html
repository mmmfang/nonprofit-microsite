One Story

Being homeless didn't stop this middle school student from making honor roll and becoming debate chamption. The City Bar Justice Center helped get her essentials such as the laptop needed for her advanced classes and warm winter clothing. After two years in a shelter, she and her mother recently moved into a private dwelling.

As profiled in The New York Times

The New York Times has featured a few of the families featured in the photo story.

After fleeing domestic violence, our client ended up homeless, in a shelter with her autistic son. Since the publication of this article, the client has moved into a two-bedroom apartment in a supportive housing building and her child has educational toys and piano keyboard to help him thrive. She continues to host a public-access TV show.

These clients came to the United States seeking better medical care for their disabled granddaughter, but ended up in the shelter system. Moved to a shelter two hours away from the granddaughter's school, but advocacy by pro bono lawyers and the City Bar Justice Center got the family transferred to a much closer shelter.The grandmother has been trained and is working as a home health aide, while their granddaugter is doing well in school.
