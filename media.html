A Special Video Focus

As profiled in The New York Times

The New York Times has featured a few of the families featured in the photo essay.

After fleeing domestic violence, our client ended up homeless, in a shelter with her autistic son. Since the publication of this article, she has moved into a two-bedroom apartment in a supportive housing building and her child has educational toys and piano keyboard to help him thrive. She continues to host a public-access TV show.

This family ended up in the shelter system after seeking better medical care for their disabled granddaughter in the U.S. Advocacy by pro bono lawyers and the City Bar Justice Center moved them to a more suitable shelter and arranged job training for the grandmother, who is now working as a home health aide. Their granddaugter is doing well in school. Link.
